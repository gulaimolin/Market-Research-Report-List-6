<p><p>A capacitive sensor is a type of electronic sensor that detects changes in capacitance caused by the proximity or touch of an object, typically a human finger. It works by measuring the capacitance between two conductive plates, which changes when an object enters the field. These sensors are widely used in touch screens, mobile devices, and various industrial applications due to their high sensitivity, durability, and versatility.</p><p>The capacitive sensor market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing adoption of touch-enabled devices, including smartphones, tablets, and wearable technology.
