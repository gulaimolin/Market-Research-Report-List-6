<p><p>The multimode optical fiber market primarily features two types: 50/125 μm and 62.5/125 μm fibers. The 50/125 μm fiber has a smaller core diameter, allowing for higher bandwidth and data transmission over shorter distances, making it suitable for data centers and high-speed networks. In contrast, the 62.5/125 μm fiber, with a larger core, supports longer distances but offers lower bandwidth, often used in legacy systems and installations. Both types serve distinct applications based on performance and distance needs.</p></p>
