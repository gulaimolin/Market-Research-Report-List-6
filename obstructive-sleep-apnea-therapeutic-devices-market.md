<p><ul><li>Positive Airway Pressure (PAP) Devices</li><li>Facial Interfaces</li><li>Adaptive Servo-Ventilation (ASV)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Positive Airway Pressure (PAP) Devices are the most commonly used therapeutic devices for obstructive sleep apnea, providing a constant stream of pressurized air through a mask to keep the airway open during sleep. Facial Interfaces refer to the various masks and headgear used with PAP devices to ensure a secure and comfortable fit. Adaptive Servo-Ventilation (ASV) devices adjust the pressure of air delivered based on the patient's breathing patterns. Other devices in the market include oral appliances, positional therapy devices, and expiratory positive airway pressure devices.</p></p>
