<p><p>The Foot Orthosis (FO) market is primarily divided into two types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to facilitate movement and provide support during physical activity, often incorporating flexible materials that adapt to the foot's motion. In contrast, static orthotics are intended to maintain foot position and alignment, offering firmness and structure to control foot motion. Both types serve different therapeutic needs, enhancing comfort and function for individuals with various foot-related conditions.</p></p>
