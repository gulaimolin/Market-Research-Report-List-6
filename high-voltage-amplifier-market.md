<p><p>The high voltage amplifier market is typically divided into unipolar and bipolar types. Unipolar amplifiers output signals in one direction, making them ideal for applications requiring simple, single-polarity operations. They are often used in systems that operate with positive voltage levels. In contrast, bipolar amplifiers can output signals in both positive and negative directions, offering greater versatility for complex signal processing applications. This dual capability makes bipolar amplifiers suitable for a wider range of electronic applications, including audio and instrumentation.</p></p>
