<p><p>Low Power Real-time Clocks (RTCs) are essential for maintaining time and date in battery-operated devices, ensuring minimal power consumption during operation. The market features primarily two types of RTCs: I2C-bus RTCs and SPI RTCs. I2C-bus RTCs utilize a two-wire interface, promoting simplicity and reduced pin count, making them ideal for space-constrained applications. In contrast, SPI RTCs offer higher data transfer speeds and greater performance in applications requiring rapid communication, catering to more demanding system requirements.</p></p>
