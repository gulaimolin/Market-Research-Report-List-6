<p><p>The flavor system market encompasses three main types: natural flavors, nature-identical flavors, and artificial flavors. Natural flavors are derived from plant or animal sources, retaining their original characteristics. Nature-identical flavors are chemically synthesized to mimic the profile of natural flavors but do not come from these sources. Artificial flavors, on the other hand, are entirely synthetic and designed to create specific tastes. The choice between these types largely depends on cost, regulatory factors, and consumer preferences for natural versus synthetic ingredients.</p></p>
