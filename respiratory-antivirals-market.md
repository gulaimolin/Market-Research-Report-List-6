<p><p>Respiratory antivirals market is divided into different types based on their mechanism of action. Nucleoside analogs work by interfering with viral genetic material replication. Neuraminidase inhibitors prevent the release of viruses from infected cells. Ion channel blockers disrupt the flow of ions necessary for viral replication. Fusion protein inhibitors prevent the fusion of viral and cellular membranes, inhibiting viral entry. Each type of antiviral targets a specific aspect of the viral life cycle, making them effective in treating respiratory infections caused by various viruses.</p></p>
