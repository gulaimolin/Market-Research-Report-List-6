<p><p>Nonlinear optical (NLO) crystals are materials that exhibit nonlinear interactions with light, essential for various applications in optics, such as frequency conversion and laser technologies. Key types include KTP (Potassium Titanyl Phosphate), known for efficient second-harmonic generation; BBO (Beta Barium Borate) with broad transparency; LBO (Lithium Triborate) for high laser damage threshold; CLBO (Cadmium Lithium Borate) with excellent phase matching; DKDP (Deuterated Potassium Di(Hydrogen) Phosphate) and KDP (Potassium Di(Hydrogen) Phosphate) for electro-optic applications; ADP (Ammonium Dihydrogen Phosphate) and others, each offering unique properties for specific optical functions.</p></p>
