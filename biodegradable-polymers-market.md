<p><p>The biodegradable polymers market is categorized into two main types: synthetic and natural biodegradable polymers. Synthetic biodegradable polymers are artificially created through chemical processes, offering tailored properties for specific applications, such as polylactic acid (PLA) and polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA). In contrast, natural biodegradable polymers are derived from natural sources, including starch, cellulose, and proteins, providing eco-friendly alternatives with inherent biodegradability. Both types are gaining traction in various industries, driven by environmental concerns and the demand for sustainable materials.</p></p>
