<p><p>The impression materials market consists of three main types: alginates, silicones, and polyethers. Alginates are economical and commonly used for preliminary impressions. Silicones offer more accuracy and detail, making them suitable for final impressions. Polyethers provide high precision and dimensional stability, making them ideal for complex cases. Each material type caters to specific needs and preferences of dental professionals, with varying cost, ease of use, and accuracy levels being key factors in their selection.</p></p>
