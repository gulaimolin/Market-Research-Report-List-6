<p><ul><li>Column</li><li>Document</li><li>Key-value</li><li>Graph</li></ul></p>
<p><p>NoSQL databases are categorized into four main types. Column stores, like Cassandra, organize data in columns for efficient read/write operations, suitable for analytics. Document stores, such as MongoDB, store data as JSON-like documents, providing flexibility in data structures. Key-value stores, exemplified by Redis, use simple key-value pairs for quick retrieval, ideal for caching and session management. Graph databases, like Neo4j, focus on relationships between entities, making them perfect for applications requiring complex queries, such as social networks and recommendation engines.</p></p>
