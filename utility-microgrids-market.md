<p><p>Utility microgrids can be categorized into three market types based on their electrical architecture. AC microgrid systems use alternating current, benefiting from established infrastructure and interoperability with the main grid. DC microgrid systems operate on direct current, offering greater efficiency for renewable sources and energy storage. Hybrid microgrid systems combine both AC and DC components, enhancing flexibility and optimizing energy distribution. Each type serves unique applications, addressing diverse energy demands and promoting resilience in local power systems.</p></p>
