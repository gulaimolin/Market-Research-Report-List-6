<p><p>The seaplane market consists of three primary types: floatplanes, flying boats, and amphibians. Floatplanes are equipped with pontoons or floats for water landing, while flying boats have a hull designed to float, allowing for water landings without additional flotation devices. Amphibians combine both features, enabling operation on both water and land. These distinct aircraft serve varied roles, from recreational flights and tourism to cargo transport and emergency services, catering to diverse needs in aquatic environments.</p></p>
