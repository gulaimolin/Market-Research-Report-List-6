<p><p>The hydrocephalus shunting valves market is primarily categorized into two types: fixed pressure valves and adjustable valves. Fixed pressure valves are designed to maintain a constant pressure, allowing cerebrospinal fluid to drain at a predetermined rate, which is effective for stable conditions. In contrast, adjustable valves enable healthcare providers to modify the pressure settings manually, making them versatile for varying patient needs and conditions. This adaptability can provide better management of intracranial pressure fluctuations, enhancing patient outcomes.</p></p>
