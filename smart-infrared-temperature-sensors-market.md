<p><p>The smart infrared temperature sensors market is divided into two main types: contact type and non-contact type. Contact type sensors require physical contact with the object to measure temperature, typically offering high accuracy and reliability. In contrast, non-contact type sensors measure temperature from a distance using infrared radiation, making them ideal for situations where contact is impractical, such as in moving objects or hazardous environments. Both types are essential across various sectors, including healthcare, manufacturing, and automation.</p></p>
