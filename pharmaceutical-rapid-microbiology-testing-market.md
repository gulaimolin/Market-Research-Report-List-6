<p><ul><li>Immunology Based Methods</li><li>Nucleic Acid Based Amplification Tests</li><li>Mass Spectrometry Based Methods</li><li>Flow Cytometry Based Methods</li><li>Growth-Based Detection Technologies</li><li>Biochemical Tests</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pharmaceutical rapid microbiology testing market encompasses various methods for detecting microbial contamination. Immunology-based methods utilize antibodies to identify pathogens quickly. Nucleic acid-based amplification tests (NAATs) amplify microbial DNA for precise detection. Mass spectrometry methods analyze microbial components for rapid identification. Flow cytometry allows for the quantification and characterization of microorganisms based on their physical and chemical properties. Growth-based detection technologies rely on culturing techniques, while biochemical tests utilize metabolic responses to determine microbial presence swiftly. Each method offers unique advantages in ensuring product safety and compliance.</p></p>
